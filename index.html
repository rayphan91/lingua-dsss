The DiSSS Framework
Tim Ferriss's method from DOAC — now with Italian & Ukrainian. Deconstruct language into minimal learnable units, select the high-leverage 1,500 words, sequence for maximum early wins, and create stakes that make quitting costly.
D · Deconstruction
Break it into Lego bricks.
Shatter the amorphous blob of "a language" into its smallest learnable units. Find the 12 diagnostic sentences. Identify your failure points before they kill your momentum.
"What are the minimal learnable units? Deconstruct the skill into Lego pieces — then find the right 20%." — Tim Ferriss, DOAC
  • Translate the 12 diagnostic sentences
  • Identify 3 core grammar failure points
  • Interview a native speaker
  • Map the sound system (phonetics)
S¹ · Selection
The 500 that build your base.
Pareto applied ruthlessly: Pareto applied ruthlessly. Master these 500 highest-frequency words and you cover the vast majority of everyday conversation — the foundation before you scale to 1,500.
"In any language you can take 1,500–2,000 words and be functionally, conversationally fluent." — Tim Ferriss
  • Complete top 100 words
  • Reach 250 words milestone
  • Complete all 500 words
  • Have first conversation with a native speaker
S² · Sequencing
Logical ≠ conventional.
Josh Waitzkin learned chess from the endgame. Ferriss learned tango by learning the female role first. The optimal learning order is almost never the conventional one.
"Starting at the endgame reveals principles that openings obscure. Invert the conventional sequence." — Tim Ferriss
  • Phonetics & sound system (week 1)
  • Top 100 nouns + verbs (weeks 2–3)
  • Core sentence structure (week 4)
  • First conversation with native speaker
S³ · Stakes
Put real skin in the game.
Motivation fades. Stakes don't. Loss aversion is 2× more powerful than the pleasure of gain. Tie your future self to today's commitment with something that genuinely hurts to lose.
"Set up stakes with real consequences. If I don't complete this by X, I will lose Y." — Tim Ferriss, DOAC
  • Announce 30-day goal publicly
  • Set up anti-charity commitment
  • Book a real conversation with native speaker
  • Weekly accountability check-in scheduled

12 Diagnostic Sentences

Cyrillic Alphabet
Ukrainian uses the Cyrillic script — 33 letters, each with a consistent sound. Learn these first and every word in the bank becomes readable within a week.
🎯 Ferriss sequencing tip: Spend 3–4 days on Cyrillic before touching vocabulary. Once you crack the code, pronunciation becomes phonetic and consistent — unlike English. Most letters learned here will also help with Russian Cyrillic later.
